## 1.1 Working Name

**Keystone**

> *Keystone — the keystone of an arch. Without it, the structure collapses.*

Meaning anchor: What holds your team together, and what happens when that stone breaks away?

## 1.2 Pitch (Hook + Subline)

> **Hook:** *"Engineering leadership runs on gut feeling. We replace it with evidence."*
>
> **Subline:** *Workforce Resilience for Engineering Leaders — know your team, simulate change, decide with confidence.*

**Use:** Pitch deck cover, website hero, outbound opener.

## 1.3 Mission Statement

> *"We help engineering leaders make people decisions with the same rigor they apply to architecture decisions."*

## 1.4 Problem

Engineering leadership makes three classes of people decisions largely on gut instinct:

| Class | Question | Current Decision Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Performance Management** | Who performs? Who is growing, who is plateauing? | Gut instinct, 1:1 notes, anecdotal evidence |
| **Risk Management** | Where are the single points of failure? Where are the knowledge silos? | Usually only visible once someone resigns |
| **Reorg / Layoff Decisions** | Who can you lose without damaging the roadmap? | Spreadsheet calendars, political optics, gut instinct |

**Result:** Bad decisions are expensive: layoffs followed by quiet rehiring; burnout among key people; growth drag caused by invisible silos; reorgs that destroy more than they organize.

## 1.5 Solution — Three-Tier Architecture

| Tier | Module | What It Delivers | Evaluation Depth | Risk Level | MVP? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Map** — Knowledge & Coverage | Topic, skill, and ownership map | Topic/coverage view, no judgment about people | Low | ✅ Yes |
| **2** | **Pulse** — Activity & Engagement | Activity density, output, engagement | Activity + output | Medium | ⏳ Phase 2 |
| **3** | **Decide** — Resilience Scoring | Reorg simulation, retention risk, decision support | Performance scoring | High | ✅ Yes (Reorg Sim only) |

**Strategic effect:** Land and expand. The bookend MVP (Map + Reorg Sim) delivers immediate value, with Pulse as the upsell. Trust escalates by tier.

## 1.6 ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

- **Persona:** VPE (org-wide) + EM (single-team) as double champion; CTO as sponsor
- **Company stage:** Series B+ tech / mid-market / public tech (50–500 engineers in phase 1)
- **Geography:** US first (phase 1), DACH/EU as phase 2
- **Pain triggers:** Reorg, layoff wave, hyper-growth, M&A integration, high attrition, roadmap friction

## 1.7 Anti-Positioning

We are **NOT**:
- a surveillance tool
- an HR system
- a productivity tracker
- a hire/fire automation tool
- a generic people analytics suite

We *are* an **engineering-native decision-support layer.**

## 1.8 Manifest — Principles

1. **Engineering decisions deserve evidence.** Architecture has reviews; people decisions have feelings. We change that.
2. **Resilience over productivity.** A "high-output" team that loses one person and breaks is not a high-output team.
3. **Decisions, not verdicts.** We provide signal; humans decide.
4. **Transparency tiers, not blanket exposure.** What ICs see ≠ what managers see ≠ what leadership sees. By design.
5. **Auditable by default.** Every output is traceable, versioned, and reproducible. AI without accountability is liability.
6. **Engineering-native, not HR-derived.** We speak Git, not headcount.

## 1.9 Category Positioning

- Industry buckets: "Engineering Intelligence," "People Analytics," "Workforce Planning"
- We position ourselves *between* them, with an **engineering-specific data foundation** (vs. HR-centric) and a **resilience lens** (vs. a productivity lens)

## 1.10 Brand Voice

- **Confident, technical, defensively positioned** (not aggressive, not watered down)
- Engineering vocabulary ("PR", "Bus Factor", "SLO"), not HR vocabulary ("Headcount", "Talent Pool")
- Resilience metaphors instead of productivity metaphors
- Provocative at the hook layer (see pitch), precise in the explanation
